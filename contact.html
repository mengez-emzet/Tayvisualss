FAQ

Questions answered.

How far in advance should I book a session?
+

We recommend booking at least 2–4 weeks in advance for standard sessions. For weddings and larger productions, we advise booking 3–6 months ahead to secure your date, especially for peak seasons. That said, if you need something sooner, reach out — we always do our best to accommodate.

Do you travel for shoots outside of Harare?
+

Absolutely. TayVisualss is available for shoots across Zimbabwe and internationally. Travel and accommodation costs for remote or international locations are discussed during the quoting process and added transparently to the project cost.

What is your turnaround time for edited photos and videos?
+

Standard turnaround is 7–10 business days for photography and 2–3 weeks for video projects. Rush delivery is available on select packages. We always communicate timelines clearly upfront so there are no surprises.

How do I receive my final images and videos?
+

All final deliverables are provided via a private, password-protected online gallery for photos and a download link for videos. You'll receive high-resolution files with full personal usage rights. Social media-optimized versions are included as standard.

What payment methods do you accept?
+

We accept Ecocash, USD cash, and international wire transfers. A non-refundable deposit (typically 30–50%) is required to confirm your booking, with the balance due on or before the shoot day.

Can I provide input on the creative direction?
+

Always. Your vision is the foundation of every project. We encourage clients to share mood boards, references, and ideas before every shoot. Our role is to take your vision and elevate it — not replace it. The best results come from genuine collaboration.

What happens if the weather is bad on our shoot day?
+

For outdoor shoots affected by severe weather, we offer a complimentary reschedule with no penalty. Light overcast conditions can actually be ideal for certain photography styles — we'll advise you on the best course of action based on the specific shoot requirements.
